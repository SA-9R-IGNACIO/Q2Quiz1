Hanfu Hanfu is from China. A traditional Chinese outfit, consists of flowing clothes, usually a long-sleeve top and a wide skirt or loose pants. It is tied at the waist with a belt and made from soft fabrics like silk or cotton.
Hanbok Hanbok is from Korea. A traditional Korean dress, consists of a short jacket called a jeogori and a long, full skirt or pants. The dress is often worn with a wide ribbon or belt to tie the jacket and waist.
Chut Thai Chut Thai is from Thailand. A traditional Thai outfit, consists of a fitted blouse or top and a long, flowing skirt called a sabai or pha nung. The outfit is usually made of silk and decorated with bright colors and patterns.
Baro't Saya Baro't Saya is from the Philippines. A traditional Filipino outfit, consists of a blouse called baro and a long skirt called saya. The blouse is usually made of light fabric, and the skirt is often made of cotton or silk.
Saree Saree is from India. A traditional Indian outfit, consists of a long piece of cloth, usually around 5 to 9 yards, wrapped around the body. The cloth is often paired with a blouse called a choli and a long skirt or petticoat underneath.
